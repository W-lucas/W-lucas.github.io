Loan Default

THE PROBLEM

A well-defined economy entails investment in the capital by giving out loans to borrowers and in return for gaining interest. The most important thing is to know the time of default of an individual in paying back the loan. Lenders who offer mortgages and other types of loans face the risk of borrowers not repaying the loan even after proper evaluation of their credit history, capacity to repay, the particular loan conditions and associated collateral. The high probability of default leads to a high risk of bad debts in which the lender may fail to get their capital back, suffer increased collection cost, disruption of cash flow and loss of interest and principal amount. In our study, we focused to estimate the time to the default of loans for males and females for a given time and the probability of default (PD). Hence, we sought to determine whether gender is a significant factor affecting the time to the default of a loan.

SOLUTION

Males had 0.7359 probability of defaulting a loan as opposed to females. We observed that the female curve decreases more than the male curve, this shows males have a higher probability of default than females. We also note that males at time 6, have a higher probability of defaulting which is 0.995.

SCOPE

The research focused on the lending institutions in which we aimed at estimating the survival probabilities and time to the default of loans for males and females. Specifically, we focused on secondary credit approval data which had a span of 72 months.

TARGET

Our research covered the finance sector, particularly the lending institutions in which we aimed at estimating the survival probabilities and time to the default of loans for males and females.
